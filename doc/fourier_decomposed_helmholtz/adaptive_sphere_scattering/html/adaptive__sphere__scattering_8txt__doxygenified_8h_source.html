20 \section scattering A specific example
21 We will solve the azimuthally Fourier-decomposed Helmholtz equation
22 \f[
23 \nabla^2 {u_{N}}(r,z) + \left(k^2-\frac{N^2}{r^2}\right) u_N(r,z) = 0,
24  \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ (1)
25 \f]
26 where \f$ N \f$ is the azimuthal wavenumber,
27 in the finite domain \f$ 1 < \sqrt{r^2 + z^2} < 3 \f$.
28 We impose the Sommerfeld radiation condition at the outer boundary
29 of the computational domain at \f$ \sqrt{r^2 + z^2} = 3\f$,
30 using a Dirichlet-to-Neumann mapping, and apply flux boundary condition
31 on the surface of the unit-sphere (where \f$ \sqrt{r^2 + z^2} = 1 \f$)
32 such that the exact solution is given by
33 \f[
34 u_N(r,z)=u_N^{[exact]}(r,z)=\sum_{l=N}^{N_{\rm terms}}
35 h_{l}^{(1)}(k\sqrt{r^2+z^2}) \ P_{l}^{N}\left(\frac{z}{\sqrt{r^2+z^2}}\right).
36 \f]
37 This solution corresponds to the superposition of several outgoing
38 waves that emerge from the unit sphere.
39 
40 The two plots below show a comparison between the exact and computed
41 solutions for \f$ N_{\rm terms}=6 \f$ , a Fourier wavenumber
42 of \f$ N=1 \f$ , and a (squared) Helmholtz wavenumber of \f$ k^2 = 10 \f$.

75 shows the main differences required to discretise the computational
76 domain with an adaptive, unstructured mesh:
77 - The provision of the functions \c actions_before/after_adapt() to
78  detach/re-attach the \c FaceElements that are used to enforce
79  the Neumann boundary conditions before and after every spatial
80  adaptation, and to pass the physical parameters to the newly
81  created bulk elements.
82  \n\n
83 - The generation of an unstructured mesh whose curvilinear boundaries are
84  represented by \c GeomObjects -- this ensures that the domain boundaries
85  become increasingly well resolved under mesh refinement.
86 .
