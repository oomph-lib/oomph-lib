52 /// \short The least-squares commutator (LSC; formerly BFBT)
53 /// preconditioner. It uses blocks corresponding to the displacement/position
54 /// and pressure unknowns, i.e. there are a total of 2x2 blocks,
55 /// and all displacement/position components are treated as a
56 /// single block of unknowns.
57 ///
58 /// Here are the details: An "ideal" preconditioner
59 /// would solve the saddle point system
60 /// \f[
61 /// \left(
62 /// \begin{array}{cc}
63 /// {\bf F} & {\bf G} \\ {\bf D} & {\bf 0}
64 /// \end{array}
65 /// \right)
66 /// \left(
67 /// \begin{array}{c}
68 /// {\bf z}_u \\ {\bf z}_p
69 /// \end{array}
70 /// \right) =
71 /// \left(
72 /// \begin{array}{c}
73 /// {\bf r}_u \\ {\bf r}_p
74 /// \end{array}
75 /// \right)
76 /// \f]
77 /// where \f$ {\bf F}\f$, \f$ {\bf G} \f$, and \f$ {\bf D}\f$ are
78 /// the blocks that arise in the Jacobian of the pressure-based
79 /// equations of linear and nonlinear elasticity (with dofs in order
80 /// of displacement/position and pressure).
81 /// The use of this preconditioner would ensure the convergence
82 /// of any iterative linear solver in a single iteration but its
83 /// application is, of course, exactly as expensive as a direct solve.
84 /// The LSC/BFBT preconditioner replaces the exact Jacobian by
85 /// a block-triangular approximation
86 /// \f[
87 /// \left(
88 /// \begin{array}{cc}
89 /// {\bf F} & {\bf G} \\ {\bf 0} & -{\bf M}_s
90 /// \end{array}
91 /// \right)
92 /// \left(
93 /// \begin{array}{c}
94 /// {\bf z}_u \\ {\bf z}_p
95 /// \end{array}
96 /// \right) =
97 /// \left(
98 /// \begin{array}{c}
99 /// {\bf r}_u \\ {\bf r}_p
100 /// \end{array}
101 /// \right),
102 /// \f]
103 /// where \f${\bf M}_s\f$ is an approximation to the pressure
104 /// Schur-complement \f$ {\bf S} = {\bf D} {\bf F}^{-1}{\bf G}. \f$
105 /// This system can be solved in two steps:
106 /// -# Solve the second row for \f$ {\bf z}_p\f$ via
107 /// \f[
108 /// {\bf z}_p = - {\bf M}_s^{-1} {\bf r}_p
109 /// \f]
110 /// -# Given \f$ {\bf z}_p \f$ , solve the first row for \f$ {\bf z}_u\f$ via
111 /// \f[
112 /// {\bf z}_u = {\bf F}^{-1} \big( {\bf r}_u - {\bf G} {\bf z}_p \big)
113 /// \f]
114 /// .
115 /// In the LSC/BFBT preconditioner, the action of the inverse pressure
116 /// Schur complement
117 /// \f[
118 /// {\bf z}_p = - {\bf M}_s^{-1} {\bf r}_p
119 /// \f]
120 /// is approximated by
121 /// \f[
122 /// {\bf z}_p = -
123 /// \big({\bf D} \widehat{\bf Q}^{-1}{\bf G} \big)^{-1}
124 /// \big({\bf D} \widehat{\bf Q}^{-1}{\bf F} \widehat{\bf Q}^{-1}{\bf G}\big)
125 /// \big({\bf D} \widehat{\bf Q}^{-1}{\bf G} \big)^{-1}
126 /// {\bf r}_p,
127 /// \f]
128 /// where \f$ \widehat{\bf Q} \f$ is the diagonal of the displacement/position
129 /// mass matrix. The evaluation of this expression involves
130 /// two linear solves involving the matrix
131 /// \f[
132 /// {\bf P} = \big({\bf D} \widehat{\bf Q}^{-1}{\bf G} \big)
133 /// \f]
134 /// which has the character of a matrix arising from the discretisation
135 /// of a Poisson problem on the pressure space. We also have
136 /// to evaluate matrix-vector products with the matrix
137 /// \f[
138 /// {\bf E}={\bf D}\widehat{\bf Q}^{-1}{\bf F}\widehat{\bf Q}^{-1}{\bf G}
139 /// \f]
140 /// Details of the theory can be found in "Finite Elements and
141 /// Fast Iterative Solvers with Applications in Incompressible Fluid
142 /// Dynamics" by Howard C. Elman, David J. Silvester, and Andrew J. Wathen,
143 /// published by Oxford University Press, 2006.
144 ///
145 /// In our implementation of the preconditioner, the linear systems
146 /// can either be solved "exactly", using SuperLU (in its incarnation
147 /// as an exact preconditioner; this is the default) or by any
148 /// other Preconditioner (inexact solver) specified via the access functions
149 /// \code
150 /// PressureBasedSolidLSCPreconditioner::set_f_preconditioner(...)
151 /// \endcode
152 /// or
153 /// \code
154 /// PressureBasedSolidLSCPreconditioner::set_p_preconditioner(...)
155 /// \endcode
