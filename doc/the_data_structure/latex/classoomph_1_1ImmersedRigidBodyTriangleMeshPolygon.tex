\doxysection{oomph\+::Immersed\+Rigid\+Body\+Triangle\+Mesh\+Polygon Class Reference}
\hypertarget{classoomph_1_1ImmersedRigidBodyTriangleMeshPolygon}{}\label{classoomph_1_1ImmersedRigidBodyTriangleMeshPolygon}\index{oomph::ImmersedRigidBodyTriangleMeshPolygon@{oomph::ImmersedRigidBodyTriangleMeshPolygon}}


Class upgrading a \doxylink{classoomph_1_1TriangleMeshPolygon}{Triangle\+Mesh\+Polygon} to a "{}hole"{} for use during triangle mesh generation. For mesh generation purposes, the main (and only) addition to the base class is the provision of the coordinates of a hole inside the polygon. To faciliate the movement of the "{}hole"{} through the domain we also provide a \doxylink{classoomph_1_1Data}{Data} object whose three values represent the x and y displacements of its centre of gravity and the polygon\textquotesingle{}s rotation about its centre of gravity. If added to a mesh in the \doxylink{classoomph_1_1Problem}{Problem} (in its incarnation as a \doxylink{classoomph_1_1GeneralisedElement}{Generalised\+Element}) the displacement/rotation of the polygon is computed in response to (i) user-\/specifiable applied forces and a torque and (ii) the net drag (and associated torque) from a mesh of elements that can exert a drag onto the polygon (typically Navier-\/\+Stokes Face\+Elements that apply a viscous drag to an immersed body, represented by the polygon.)

\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Class upgrading a \doxylink{classoomph_1_1TriangleMeshPolygon}{Triangle\+Mesh\+Polygon} to a "{}hole"{} for use during triangle mesh generation. For mesh generation purposes, the main (and only) addition to the base class is the provision of the coordinates of a hole inside the polygon. To faciliate the movement of the "{}hole"{} through the domain we also provide a \doxylink{classoomph_1_1Data}{Data} object whose three values represent the x and y displacements of its centre of gravity and the polygon\textquotesingle{}s rotation about its centre of gravity. If added to a mesh in the \doxylink{classoomph_1_1Problem}{Problem} (in its incarnation as a \doxylink{classoomph_1_1GeneralisedElement}{Generalised\+Element}) the displacement/rotation of the polygon is computed in response to (i) user-\/specifiable applied forces and a torque and (ii) the net drag (and associated torque) from a mesh of elements that can exert a drag onto the polygon (typically Navier-\/\+Stokes Face\+Elements that apply a viscous drag to an immersed body, represented by the polygon.)
