3 \mainpage Demo problem: The (axisymmetric) static free surface bounding a layer of viscous fluid.
4 
5 \section overview Overview of the problem
6 
7  We consider an open circular container of unit radius
8  containing a still viscous fluid of
9  prescribed volume \f$ \mathcal{V} \f$
10  that meets the wall of the container at a contact
11  angle \f$ \theta_{c} \f$. The problem is extremely similar to that
12  described in the
13  <a href="../../../navier_stokes/static_single_layer/html/index.html">
14  two-dimensional static cap tutorial. </a> The exact solution
15  corresponds to a free surface of constant curvature that
16  is the arc of a circle
17  rotated about the axis to give a section of a sphere.
18  The mean curvature of
19  the interface in this problem is \f$ \kappa = 2/r = 4\cos\theta_{c}
20  \f$, which differs from the two-dimensional problem in which it was \f$
21  2\cos\theta_{c} \f$.
22 
23  The only differences between the axisymmetric and the
24  <a href="../../../navier_stokes/static_single_layer/html/index.html">
25  two-dimensional </a> driver codes are that:
26 -# two-dimensional elements are replaced by the equivalent axisymmetric elements;\n
27 -# specified volume is different;\n
28 -# swirl (theta) component of velocity is pinned; \n
29 -# analytic pressure drop is changed. \n
30 .
31 
32 In other words we make the following changes:

77 \subsection com Comments
78 
79 - The formula for the specified volume in the axisymmetric
80 case is the true volume \f$ \mathcal{V} \f$ divided by \f$ 2\pi \f$ because
81 all the axisymmetric equations are divided by the common factor
82 of \f$ 2 \pi \f$. The \c AxisymmetricVolumeConstraintBoundingElement
83 class must be used so that the volume is correctly calculated.
84 
85 - The swirl velocity is pinned on the boundaries by including the
86 additional code
87 \code
88  Bulk_mesh_pt->boundary_node_pt(b,n)->pin(2);
89 \endcode
90 .

98  that the pressure difference is unaffected by the choice of
99  reference pressure.\n\n
100 -# Investigate what happens when the two-dimensional volume constraint
