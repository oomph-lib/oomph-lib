5 The \c oomph-lib "architects" are (in no particular order)

10 ...assisted by former/current project/MSc/PhD students and
11 collaborators who made
12 (or are still making) significant contributions to the development of
13 the library (listed in reverse chronological order):
14 - \b Patrick \b Keuchel worked on forced oscillations and resonances
15  in axisymmetric fluid-conveying tubes.
16 - \b Ben \b Gavan worked on mesh adaptation procedures using gmsh.
17 - \b Rupinder \b Matharu worked on the simulation of creep processes
18  during the annealing of HDPE materials.
19 - \b Aidan \b Retallick works on the simulation of graphene-based
20  pressure transducers.
21 - \b Christian \b Vaquero-Stainer did his Masters project on modelling
22  fingering in Hele-Shaw cells with time-dependent gap-widths, and then
23  moved on to this PhD on the subtraction of singularities in the flow
24  past arbitrarily-shaped disk-like objects in Stokes flow.
25 - \b David \b Robinson developed and implemented C1 continuous
26  triangular elements for the solution of fourth-order PDES. He used
27  them for the implementation of the Foeppl-von-Karman and Koiter-Steigman
28  plate theories.
29 - <b>Thomas Brion, Simon Finney and Hannah Chamberlain</b> all worked
30  on Foeppl-von-Karman based models of graphene-based microphones.
31 - \b Louis \b Calot-Plaetevoet improved the methodology used to
32  transfer solutions between different meshes.
33 - \b Thierry \b Gonon implemented the methodology to subtract
34  singular (or non-singular) functions off solutions to the Poisson
35  and Navier-Stokes equations.
36 - \b Chris \b Johnson has provided many bug fixes.
37 - \b Puneet \b Matharu works on the implementation of geometric
38  multigrid solvers, particularly for Helmholtz equations. He then did
39  his PhD on exotic wakes in the flow past circular cylinders and has
40  become one of the maintainers of oomph-lib,
41 - \b Chihebeddine \b Hammami worked on the implementation of
42  Yulii Shikhmurzaev's interface formation theory.
43 - \b Narjes \b Akriche worked on pseudo-resonances in acoustic
44  fluid-structure interaction problems.
45 - \b Aman \b Rajvardhan worked on implementing surfactant transport
46  equations in two- and three-dimensional geometries.
47 - \b Jordan \b Rosso worked on topological fluid mechanics of the
48  the Karman vortex street.
49 - \b Florian \b Molinier did some early work on the coupled solution
50  of the axisymmetric free-surface Navier-Stokes equations and
51  the axisymmetric Foeppl von Karman equations.
52 - \b Jonathan \b Deakin worked on a glaciology-related melt problem
53  (and has since returned as PhD student to work on the numerical
54  solution of acoustic fluid-structure interaction problems and
55  optimal PML methods. He has since become one of the maintainers of oomph-lib.
56 - \b Draga \b Pihler-Puzovic worked on the the coupled solution of the
57  Foeppl von Karman equations and the Reynolds lubrication equation
58  to model wrinkling/fingering in elastic-walled Hele-Shaw cells.
59 - \b Joris \b Ferrand worked on the solution of the Foeppl von Karman
60  equations.
61 - \b Harsh \b Ranjan worked on multiple solutions of Navier--Stokes flows in curved tubes.
62 - \b Anton \b Martinsson implemented the machinery required to
63  output \c oomph-lib data in paraview format, bypassing the need
64  for running the time-consuming tecplot to paraview conversion scripts.
65  He also implemented the displacement-based axisymmetric Foeppl von
66  Karman equations.
67 - \b Andr&eacute; \b Von \b Borries is working on free-surface Navier--Stokes and
68  lubrication theory problems.
69 - \b Matthew \b Walker implemented PML methods for the azimuthally
70  Fourier-decomposed Helmholtz equations.
71 - \b Joris \b Ferrand implemented the axisymmetric Foeppl von Karman
72  equations.
73 - \b Philippe \b Mesnard worked on acoustic FSI problems and introduced many
74  improvements to \c oomph-lib's machinery for handling such problems.
75 - \b Florian \b Molinier worked on the coupling of the free surface
76  Navier-Stokes equations and the axisymmetric Foeppl von Karman
77  equations (in the context of simulating flows in elastic-walled
78  Hele-Shaw problems).
79 - \b David \b Nigro developed and implemented much of the machinery
80  for acoustic fluid-structure interaction problems.
81 - \b Matthew \b Russell implemented the Foeppl-von-Karman equations;
82  he now continues to work on poro-elastic FSI problems.
83 - \b Raphael \b Perillat worked on the simulation of flows in
84  elastic-walled Hele-Shaw cells.
85 - \b Robert \b Harter works on acoustic fluid-structure interaction
86  problems.
87 - \b Radu \b Cimpeanu implemented the PML boundary conditions for the
88  Helmholtz equations and the time-harmonic equations of linear
89  elasticity.
90 - \b Julio \b Perez \b Sansalvador works on parallel unstructured
91  mesh adaptation.
92 - \b David \b Shepherd works on the numerical solution of
93  micromagnetic problems.
94 - \b Ray \b White is working on block preconditioners.
95 - \b Nico \b Bergemann made significant
96  contributions to the adaptive unstructured mesh (re-)generation
97  capabilities for free-surface problems. He then did his PhD on
98  the simulation of viscous and visco-plastic free surface flows.
99 - \b Ben \b Saxby works on hp adaptivity and XFEM.
100 - \b Michael \b Crabb worked on Discontinuous Galerkin (DG) methods.
101 - \b Peter \b Ashcroft worked on eigenvalue problems.
102 - \b Jeremy \b van \b Chu contributed to the completion the tecplot to paraview
103  conversion scripts and significantly extended the
104  <a href="../../paraview/html/index.html">the paraview tutorial.</a>
105  He also developed the \c LineVisualiser machinery (which allows
106  the extraction of computational data along lines in a
107  higher-dimensional domain) and wrote the domain-based tube mesh.
108 - \b Guilherme \b Rocha developed elements to simulate Hele-Shaw
109  problems (by solving the free-surface Reynolds lubrication
110  equations).
111 - \b Ahmed \b Wassfi extended \b Tarak \b Kharrat's work on the Helmholtz
112  equation and implemented the Fourier-decomposed version of this
113  equation.
114 - \b Alexandre \b Raczynski keeps providing bug fixes and contributed
115  to the completion the tecplot to paraview conversion scripts
116  discussed in the <a href="../../paraview/html/index.html">the
117  paraview tutorial.</a>
118 - \b David \b Rutter wrote the
119  <a href="../../linear_elasticity/periodic_load/html/index.html">
120  tutorial for the linear elasticity equations.</a>
121 - \b Tarak \b Kharrat implemented the Helmholtz elements and the
122  methodology to apply the Sommerfeld radiation condition.
123 - \b Luigi \b Collucci continued Benjamin Metz's work and developed the
124  interface from \c oomph-lib to \c Triangle.
125 - \b Francisco \b Jose \b Blanco \b Rodriguez worked on free-surface
126  problems and wrote the driver code that simulates the Rayleigh
127  instability of an axisymmetric jet.
128 - \b Wassamon \b Phusakulkajorn worked on C1-continuous triangular
129  finite elements for shell, beam and biharmonic problems.
130 - \b Benjamin \b Metz worked on adaptivity and solution transfer for
131  unstructured meshes.
132 - \b Amine \b Massit worked on outflow boundary conditions for
133  Navier-Stokes problems and physiological FSI problems based
134  on meshes generated by <a href="http://www.vmtk.org/">vmtk</a>.
135 - \b Patrick \b Hurley works on free surface Navier-Stokes problems.
136 - <B>Andy Gait</B> worked on parallelisation, in particular
137  the problem distribution and the subsequent distributed mesh adaptation.
138 - <B>Angelo
139  Simone</B> wrote python scripts that convert \c oomph-lib's
140  output to the vtu format that can be read by
141  <a href="http://www.paraview.org">paraview</a>;
142  see <a href="../../paraview/html/index.html">the paraview tutorial</a>
143  for details.
144 - \b Sophie \b Kershaw worked on the Navier-Stokes equations
145  in spherical coordinates.
146 - \b Floraine \b Cordier developed the driver codes and tutorials for the
147  <a href="../../interaction/fsi_channel_with_leaflet/html/index.html">
148  flow past the elastic leaflet</a> and
149  <a href="../../interaction/turek_flag/html/index.html">
150  Turek & Hron's FSI benchmark</a>. In the process she significantly
151  extended \c oomph-lib's FSI capabilities.
152 - <B> Stefan Kollmannsberger</B> and his students Iason Papaioannou and
153  Orkun Oezkan Doenmez developed early versions of the code
154  for <a href="../../interaction/turek_flag/html/index.html">
155  Turek & Hron's FSI benchmark</a> and its
156  <a href="../../navier_stokes/turek_flag_non_fsi/html/index.html">
157  non-FSI counterpart.</a>
158 - <B>Cedric Ody</B> developed the \c YoungLaplace elements and their refineable
159  counterparts to study capillary statics problems.
160 - \b Alice \b Gaertig developed interfaces to the third-party mesh
161  generators <A HREF="http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~quake/triangle.html">
162  \c Triangle, </A> <A HREF="http://wias-berlin.de/software/tetgen//">
163  \c TetGen, </A>, <A HREF="http://members.shaw.ca/bjoe/">\c
164  Geompack++, </A> and <A HREF="http://www.dimap.ufrn.br/~mfsiqueira/Marcelo_Siqueiras_Web_Spot/cqmesh.html">
165  \c CQMesh. </A>
166 - \b Claire \b Blancon developed the demo drivers for the collapsible
167  channel problem (with and without fluid-structure interaction).
168 - \b Nick \b Chapman worked on the implementation of triangular
169  and tet-elements.
170 - \b Chris \b Gold implemented explicit timestepping
171  schemes.
172 - \b Phil \b Haines worked on bifurcation detection and tracking for
173  the Navier-Stokes equations and developed the formulation of
174  the equations in plane polar coordinates.
175 - \b Richard \b Muddle worked on the block preconditioning techniques for
176  the biharmonic (and many other) equations, and parallel solvers.
177 - \b Glyn \b Rees worked on iterative linear solvers and multigrid
178 - \b Alberto \b de \b Lozar worked on 3D free-surface Navier-Stokes problems.
179 - \b Jonathan \b Boyle developed the initial interfaces to third-party
180  iterative solvers and is now involved in the further parallelisation of the
181  code and the implementation and application of block-preconditioning
182  techniques for Navier-Stokes and fluid-structure interaction problems.
183 - \b Renaud \b Schleck completed the octree-based mesh refinement procedures
184  and wrote the MPI-based parallel assembly routines and the

187  spectral elements.

189  Navier-Stokes problems, with and without free surfaces, and developed

192 - \b Jean-Michel \b Lenoir implemented the first part of the
193  octree-based 3D mesh refinement procedures.
