5 The \c oomph-lib "architects" are (in no particular order)

10 ...assisted by former/current project/MSc/PhD students and
11 collaborators who made
12 (or are still making) significant contributions to the development of
13 the library (listed in reverse chronological order):
14 - \b Thierry \b Gonon implemented the methodology to subtract
15  singular (or non-singular) functions off solutions to the Poisson
16  and Navier-Stokes equations.
17 - \b Chris \b Johnson has provided many bug fixes.
18 - \b Puneet \b Matharu works on the implementation of geometric
19  multigrid solvers, particularly for Helmholtz equations.
20 - \b Chihebeddine \b Hammami worked on the implementation of
21  Yulii Shikhmurzaev's interface formation theory.
22 - \b Narjes \b Akriche worked on pseudo-resonances in acoustic
23  fluid-structure interaction problems.
24 - \b Aman \b Rajvardhan worked on implementing surfactant transport
25  equations in two- and three-dimensional geometries.
26 - \b Jordan \b Rosso worked on topological fluid mechanics of the
27  the Karman vortex street.
28 - \b Florian \b Molinier did some early work on the coupled solution
29  of the axisymmetric free-surface Navier-Stokes equations and
30  the axisymmetric Foeppl von Karman equations.
31 - \b Jonathan \b Deakin worked on a glaciology-related melt problem
32  (and has since returned as PhD student to work on the numerical
33  solution of acoustic fluid-structure interaction problems).
34 - \b Draga \b Pihler-Puzovic worked on the the coupled solution of the
35  Foeppl von Karman equations and the Reynolds lubrication equation
36  to model wrinkling/fingering in elastic-walled Hele-Shaw cells.
37 - \b Joris \b Ferrand worked on the solution of the Foeppl von Karman
38  equations.
39 - \b Harsh \b Ranjan worked on multiple solutions of Navier--Stokes flows in curved tubes.
40 - \b Anton \b Martinsson implemented the machinery required to
41  output \c oomph-lib data in paraview format, bypassing the need
42  for running the time-consuming tecplot to paraview conversion scripts.
43  He also implemented the displacement-based axisymmetric Foeppl von
44  Karman equations.
45 - \b Andr&eacute; \b Von \b Borries is working on free-surface Navier--Stokes and
46  lubrication theory problems.
47 - \b Matthew \b Walker implemented PML methods for the azimuthally
48  Fourier-decomposed Helmholtz equations.
49 - \b Joris \b Ferrand implemented the axisymmetric Foeppl von Karman
50  equations.
51 - \b Philippe \b Mesnard worked on acoustic FSI problems and introduced many
52  improvements to \c oomph-lib's machinery for handling such problems.
53 - \b Florian \b Molinier worked on the coupling of the free surface
54  Navier-Stokes equations and the axisymmetric Foeppl von Karman
55  equations (in the context of simulating flows in elastic-walled
56  Hele-Shaw problems).
57 - \b David \b Nigro developed and implemented much of the machinery
58  for acoustic fluid-structure interaction problems.
59 - \b Matthew \b Russell implemented the Foeppl-von-Karman equations;
60  he now continues to work on poro-elastic FSI problems.
61 - \b Raphael \b Perillat worked on the simulation of flows in
62  elastic-walled Hele-Shaw cells.
63 - \b Robert \b Harter works on acoustic fluid-structure interaction
64  problems.
65 - \b Radu \b Cimpeanu implemented the PML boundary conditions for the
66  Helmholtz equations and the time-harmonic equations of linear
67  elasticity.
68 - \b Julio \b Perez \b Sansalvador works on parallel unstructured
69  mesh adaptation.
70 - \b David \b Shepherd works on the numerical solution of
71  micromagnetic problems.
72 - \b Ray \b White is working on block preconditioners.
73 - \b Nico \b Bergemann made (and continues to make) significant
74  contributions to the adaptive unstructured mesh (re-)generation
75  capabilities for free-surface problems.
76 - \b Ben \b Saxby works on hp adaptivity and XFEM.
77 - \b Michael \b Crabb worked on Discontinuous Galerkin (DG) methods.
78 - \b Peter \b Ashcroft worked on eigenvalue problems.
79 - \b Jeremy \b van \b Chu contributed to the completion the tecplot to paraview
80  conversion scripts and significantly extended the
81  <a href="../../paraview/html/index.html">the paraview tutorial.</a>
82  He also developed the \c LineVisualiser machinery (which allows
83  the extraction of computational data along lines in a
84  higher-dimensional domain) and wrote the domain-based tube mesh.
85 - \b Guilherme \b Rocha developed elements to simulate Hele-Shaw
86  problems (by solving the free-surface Reynolds lubrication
87  equations).
88 - \b Ahmed \b Wassfi extended \b Tarak \b Kharrat's work on the Helmholtz
89  equation and implemented the Fourier-decomposed version of this
90  equation.
91 - \b Alexandre \b Raczynski keeps providing bug fixes and contributed
92  to the completion the tecplot to paraview conversion scripts
93  discussed in the <a href="../../paraview/html/index.html">the
94  paraview tutorial.</a>
95 - \b David \b Rutter wrote the
96  <a href="../../linear_elasticity/periodic_load/html/index.html">
97  tutorial for the linear elasticity equations.</a>
98 - \b Tarak \b Kharrat implemented the Helmholtz elements and the
99  methodology to apply the Sommerfeld radiation condition.
100 - \b Luigi \b Collucci continued Benjamin Metz's work and developed the
101  interface from \c oomph-lib to \c Triangle.
102 - \b Francisco \b Jose \b Blanco \b Rodriguez worked on free-surface
103  problems and wrote the driver code that simulates the Rayleigh
104  instability of an axisymmetric jet.
105 - \b Wassamon \b Phusakulkajorn worked on C1-continuous triangular
106  finite elements for shell, beam and biharmonic problems.
107 - \b Benjamin \b Metz worked on adaptivity and solution transfer for
108  unstructured meshes.
109 - \b Amine \b Massit worked on outflow boundary conditions for
110  Navier-Stokes problems and physiological FSI problems based
111  on meshes generated by <a href="http://www.vmtk.org/">vmtk</a>.
112 - \b Patrick \b Hurley works on free surface Navier-Stokes problems.
113 - <B>Andy Gait</B> worked on parallelisation, in particular
114  the problem distribution and the subsequent distributed mesh adaptation.
115 - <B>Angelo
116  Simone</B> wrote python scripts that convert \c oomph-lib's
117  output to the vtu format that can be read by
118  <a href="http://www.paraview.org">paraview</a>;
119  see <a href="../../paraview/html/index.html">the paraview tutorial</a>
120  for details.
121 - \b Sophie \b Kershaw worked on the Navier-Stokes equations
122  in spherical coordinates.
123 - \b Floraine \b Cordier developed the driver codes and tutorials for the
124  <a href="../../interaction/fsi_channel_with_leaflet/html/index.html">
125  flow past the elastic leaflet</a> and
126  <a href="../../interaction/turek_flag/html/index.html">
127  Turek & Hron's FSI benchmark</a>. In the process she significantly
128  extended \c oomph-lib's FSI capabilities.
129 - <B> Stefan Kollmannsberger</B> and his students Iason Papaioannou and
130  Orkun Oezkan Doenmez developed early versions of the code
131  for <a href="../../interaction/turek_flag/html/index.html">
132  Turek & Hron's FSI benchmark</a> and its
133  <a href="../../navier_stokes/turek_flag_non_fsi/html/index.html">
134  non-FSI counterpart.</a>
135 - <B>Cedric Ody</B> developed the \c YoungLaplace elements and their refineable
136  counterparts to study capillary statics problems.
137 - \b Alice \b Gaertig developed interfaces to the third-party mesh
138  generators <A HREF="http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~quake/triangle.html">
139  \c Triangle, </A> <A HREF="http://wias-berlin.de/software/tetgen//">
140  \c TetGen, </A>, <A HREF="http://members.shaw.ca/bjoe/">\c
141  Geompack++, </A> and <A HREF="http://www.dimap.ufrn.br/~mfsiqueira/Marcelo_Siqueiras_Web_Spot/cqmesh.html">
142  \c CQMesh. </A>
143 - \b Claire \b Blancon developed the demo drivers for the collapsible
144  channel problem (with and without fluid-structure interaction).
145 - \b Nick \b Chapman worked on the implementation of triangular
146  and tet-elements.
147 - \b Chris \b Gold implemented explicit timestepping
148  schemes.
149 - \b Phil \b Haines worked on bifurcation detection and tracking for
150  the Navier-Stokes equations and developed the formulation of
151  the equations in plane polar coordinates.
152 - \b Richard \b Muddle worked on the block preconditioning techniques for
153  the biharmonic (and many other) equations, and parallel solvers.
154 - \b Glyn \b Rees worked on iterative linear solvers and multigrid
155 - \b Alberto \b de \b Lozar worked on 3D free-surface Navier-Stokes problems.
156 - \b Jonathan \b Boyle developed the initial interfaces to third-party
157  iterative solvers and is now involved in the further parallelisation of the
158  code and the implementation and application of block-preconditioning
159  techniques for Navier-Stokes and fluid-structure interaction problems.
160 - \b Renaud \b Schleck completed the octree-based mesh refinement procedures
161  and wrote the MPI-based parallel assembly routines and the
162  interfaces to SuperLU_dist.
163 - \b Sharaf \b Al-Sharif provided the initial implementation of nodal
164  spectral elements.
165 - \b Daniel \b Meyer used oomph-lib to study a variety of axisymmetric
166  Navier-Stokes problems, with and without free surfaces, and developed
167  drafts for many of our tutorials.
168 - \b Alexandre \b Klimowicz worked on block-preconditioning methods.
169 - \b Jean-Michel \b Lenoir implemented the first part of the
170  octree-based 3D mesh refinement procedures.
171 - \b Gemma \b Barson provided the initial implementation for the 2D Delaunay
172  mesh generation procedures.
173 
174 We're always looking for more help! Get in touch if you're interested
175 in joining the team.
