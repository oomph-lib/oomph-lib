3 \mainpage The spatially-adaptive solution of the azimuthally Fourier-decomposed equations of 3D time-harmonic linear elasticity on unstructured meshes
4 
5 In this tutorial we re-visit the solution of the
6 time-harmonic equations of 3D linear elasticity in cylindrical polar
7 coordinates, using a Fourier decomposition of the solution in the
8 azimuthal direction. The driver code is very similar to the one discussed in
9 <a href="../../cylinder/html/index.html">another tutorial
10 </a> -- the main purpose of the current tutorial is to demonstrate
11 the use of spatial adaptivity on unstructured meshes. Compared to
12 the test case considered in the <a href="../../cylinder/html/index.html">
13 other tutorial</a> we study a slightly less contrived test problem:
14 the forced time-harmonic oscillations of a finite-length, hollow cylinder,
15 loaded by a time-periodic pressure load on its inner surface.

38 \section test The test problem
39 The figure below shows the problem considered in this tutorial:
40 an annular elastic body that occupies the region \f$ r_{\rm min}\leq
41 r\leq r_{\rm max}, z_{\rm min}\leq
42 z\leq z_{\rm max} \f$ is loaded by
43 a time-harmonic pressure load acting on its inner surface
44 (at \f$ r = r_{\rm min} \f$). The upper and lower ends of the
45 hollow cylinder (at \f$ z = z_{\rm min} \f$ and \f$ z = z_{\rm min} \f$)
46 are held at a fixed position.

58 \section num The numerical solution
59 The driver code for this problem is very similar to the one discussed in
60 <a href=../../cylinder/html/index.html>another tutorial</a>.
61 Running \c sdiff on the two driver codes
62 <CENTER>
63 <A HREF="../../../../demo_drivers/time_harmonic_fourier_decomposed_linear_elasticity/cylinder/cylinder.cc">
64 demo_drivers/time_harmonic_fourier_decomposed_linear_elasticity/cylinder/cylinder.cc
65 </A>
66 </CENTER>
67 and
68 <CENTER>
69 <A HREF="../../../../demo_drivers/time_harmonic_fourier_decomposed_linear_elasticity/cylinder/pressure_loaded_cylinder.cc">
70 demo_drivers/time_harmonic_fourier_decomposed_linear_elasticity/cylinder/pressure_loaded_cylinder.cc
71 </A>
72 </CENTER>
73 shows you the differences, the most important of which are:
74 - The change of the forcing to a spatially constant
75  pressure load on the inside boundary.
76 - The provision of the \c actions_before/after_adapt() functions and a
77  helper function \c complete_problem_setup()
78  which rebuilds the elements (by passing the problem parameters
79  to the elements) following the unstructured mesh adaptation.
80  (The need/rationale for such a function is discussed in
81  <a href="../../../meshes/mesh_from_inline_triangle/html/index.html">
82  another tutorial.</a>)
83 - The mesh generation and the application of boundary conditions at
84  the upper and lower boundaries of the hollow cylinder.
85 .
86 All of this is reasonably straightforward and provides a
87 powerful code that automatically adapts the mesh in regions of
88 large displacement gradients. Have a look through the driver code
89 and play with it.
